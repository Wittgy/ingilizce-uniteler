Grammar Topics

⭐ Present Perfect Tense (Have / Has + V3)

We use Present Perfect to talk about life experiences. We do NOT say when it happened.

Positive (+)

I / You / We / They → have + V3 He / She / It → has + V3
✈️ I have been to Italy. | She has visited many countries.

Negative (–)

haven't + V3 hasn't + V3
❌ I have never tried chocolate pizza. | He hasn't been abroad.

Question (?)

Have / Has + subject + V3?
❓ Have you ever been to Spain? | Has she visited Japan?

⏰ Time Expressions

ever → in questions   never → negative

already = before now   just = a short time ago

yet = until now (questions & negatives)

for + period of time (for 2 years)
since + starting point (since 2022)
📘 I have already finished my homework.
📘 She has just arrived home.
📘 Have you eaten breakfast yet?
📘 I have lived here for two years.
📘 She has studied English since 2022.

⚖️ Quantifiers (Miktar İfadeleri)

First, know if a noun is Countable (sayılabilir) or Uncountable (sayılamaz)!

Countable: apple, egg, carrot, burger, tomato
Uncountable: sugar, flour, meat, water, salt, milk, bread, rice, cheese

HOW MANY / HOW MUCH

How many + countable → How many apples?
How much + uncountable → How much milk?

SOME / ANY

some → positive sentences & offers (I want some eggs.)
any → questions & negatives (Is there any milk?)

A FEW / A LITTLE

a few + countable plural (a few pears)
a little + uncountable (a little bread)

MANY / MUCH / A LOT OF

many + countable | much + uncountable
a lot of + both (a lot of milk / a lot of eggs)

TOO MANY / TOO MUCH / ENOUGH

too many + countable = too many cars
too much + uncountable = too much sugar
enough = yeterli (enough money / enough chairs)

BOTH / EITHER / NEITHER

both … and = her ikisi de (Both water and milk are good.)
either … or = ya … ya (Either water or juice.)
neither … nor = ne … ne (Neither coffee nor tea.)
