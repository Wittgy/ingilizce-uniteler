Grammar Topics

📏 Comparatives & Superlatives

Use comparatives to compare two things. Use superlatives for the most/best in a group.

Short adjectives (1 syllable)

big → bigger → the biggest fast → faster → the fastest

➕ Add -er for comparative | Add -est for superlative
🌍 Earth is bigger than Mars.
🪐 Jupiter is the biggest planet.

-y adjectives → change y to i

heavy → heavier → the heaviest

Long adjectives (2+ syllables)

more dangerous the most dangerous

➕ Use more and the most
🧑‍🚀 Space is more dangerous than Earth.

⚠️ Irregular forms

good → better → the best bad → worse → the worst

🔀 If & Unless

Zero Conditional – always true facts

If + Simple Present → Simple Present
(Scientific facts, nature facts)
❄️ If water freezes, it turns into ice.
🌙 If there is no atmosphere, life does not exist.

First Conditional – possible future

If + Simple Present → will + verb
(Future predictions, warnings, advice)
♻️ If we recycle, the Earth will be cleaner.
🌡️ If climate change continues, extreme weather will increase.

Unless = "If… not"

Unless + Simple Present → will + verb
🦁 Unless we protect nature, animals will die.
👨‍🚀 Unless astronauts wear spacesuits, they will freeze.

🔧 Modal Verbs

CAN / CAN'T – ability, possibility

can + verb | can't + verb
🚀 Astronauts can travel into space.
😮 Humans can't live on the Moon.

MUST / MUSTN'T – rules & obligation

must + verb = you have to do it
mustn't + verb = it is forbidden!
👨‍🚀 Astronauts must wear a spacesuit.
🚫 We mustn't pollute the Earth.

SHOULD / SHOULDN'T – advice

should + verb = it is a good idea
shouldn't + verb = not a good idea
💡 We should save energy.
💧 We shouldn't waste water.
