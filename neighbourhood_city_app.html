⏰ Past Simple: WAS / WERE

We use was / were to talk about where someone was in the past.

Subject – Verb Table

I / He / She / It → was You / We / They → were
📍 I was at the bus stop yesterday.
🏛️ They were near the museum last week.

Negative Forms

was not → wasn't were not → weren't
😐 She wasn't at the park after school.
😐 We weren't in the city centre last night.

Questions

Was / Were + subject + place?
❓ Was she at home last night?
❓ Were they near the museum?

📚 Past Simple: Regular & Irregular Verbs

Regular Verbs → add -ed

play → played walk → walked visit → visited wait → waited listen → listened watch → watched
🎸 John played the guitar last night.

-y endings → change y to i + ed

try → tried cry → cried

Irregular Verbs

go → went see → saw come → came read → read have → had buy → bought
🛒 Lucy went to the library last Saturday.

Negative: didn't + verb (base form)

Subject + didn't + verb
📺 Grandpa didn't watch TV last night.

Questions: Did + subject + verb?

❓ Did Jane like the party?

📍 Prepositions of Place

Use these to say where something is.

in – inside on – on top of (touching) under – below next to – beside behind – at the back in front of – at the front between – in the middle of two near – close to opposite – across from over – above (not touching)
🏦 The bank is between the cinema and the grocery.
🐕 The dog is in front of the doghouse.
🚌 The bus stop is opposite the cinema.

🕐 Past Time Expressions

yesterday last night last week last month two days ago five years ago yesterday morning yesterday evening
🗓️ Tom went to the library last Saturday.
🗓️ I finished my homework last night.
